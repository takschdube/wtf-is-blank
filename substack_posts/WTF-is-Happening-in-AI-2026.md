We&#8217;re back! Hope your holidays were restful.My advisor emailed on January 2nd asking about my &#8220;2026 publication goals.&#8221; I responded with an AI-generated motivational poster. We&#8217;re not speaking.So. 2026. The year every 2023 prediction said we&#8217;d have robot butlers and fully autonomous everything. Instead we have AI that passes the bar exam but can&#8217;t count the letters in &#8220;strawberry.&#8221;Let&#8217;s do a quick vibe check on where we actually are, make some spicy predictions, and revisit this in January 2027 to see how wrong I was.2025: The TL;DRWhat actually shipped:60% of Tier-1 support tickets now handled by AI at major companiesGitHub says 46% of code in enabled repos is AI-generatedEvery model got vision. And audio. And video (sort of).Agents went from &#8220;cool demo&#8221; to &#8220;occasionally deletes production databases&#8221;What actually broke:Deloitte cited fake academics. Twice. In government reports.AI-generated spam up 900%Deepfake fraud up 3,000%Multiple &#8220;unlimited&#8221; AI plans learned that users will in fact use unlimited thingsThe vibe shift:&#8220;Just make it bigger&#8221; stopped working as wellReasoning models (o1, o3) actually made AI good at mathOpen source got genuinely competitive (Llama 3.3 &#8776; GPT-4o)The gap between models shrunk. GPT-5 vs Claude Opus 4.5 vs Gemini 3? Pick one, they&#8217;re all good.My HOT Takes (2026)1. The Models Are Commodities NowGPT-5, Claude Opus 4.5, and Gemini 3 Pro are basically interchangeable for 90% of use cases.Yeah, one is 2% better on some benchmark. Nobody cares. They all write good code, summarize documents, and occasionally hallucinate with equal confidence.The moat isn&#8217;t the model anymore. It&#8217;s distribution (OpenAI&#8217;s 300M users), integration (Copilot in every IDE), and data (your proprietary stuff).Stop obsessing over which model is marginally better. Build your product.2. Reasoning Models Are Incredible(y Expensive!)o1/o3 and friends genuinely solved the &#8220;LLMs can&#8217;t do math&#8221; problem. Complex logic, multi-step planning, actual reasoning!!!The catch: It costs 10-100x more. Your $0.01 query becomes $0.50. A &#8220;thinking&#8221; model thinking too hard can burn $5 on a single request.The move: Route 90% of queries to cheap models. Reasoning models for hard problems only. Most teams do the opposite.3. Agents Are Almost Ready&#8220;Almost&#8221; doing a lot of work in that sentence.They can reliably: book flights, manage calendars, handle defined workflows, write and test simple code.They still can&#8217;t: know when to stop, avoid catastrophic mistakes, or resist deleting your database when you explicitly tell them not to.2026 prediction: Agents become production-ready for narrow, well-defined tasks with human oversight. Not for &#8220;just figure it out&#8221; autonomy.4. Open Source Actually Won?Llama 3.3 70B matches GPT-4o. Qwen3 beats it on code. DeepSeek came out of nowhere with reasoning models at 1/10th the price.Running 70B locally:Hardware: $2,500 (Mac Studio) or $3,500 (RTX 5090 build)Monthly cost: ~$50 electricityBreak-even vs API: 3-4 months at 100K queriesThe trade: You own everything. You&#8217;re also responsible for everything. Choose your pain.5. Regulation Is Real NowEU AI Act is live. Fines up to 7% of global revenue. &#8220;We didn&#8217;t know the AI would do that&#8221; is not a legal defense.Most startups: ignoring it. Most enterprises: over-complying out of fear. The smart play: somewhere in between.AI 2026 Bingo (NOT)These are specific enough to be falsifiable. We&#8217;re revisiting this in January 2027.My (un)solicited advice.If you&#8217;re building AI products:Stop A/B testing GPT-5 vs Claude. Pick one. Ship.Build evals before you need them. The teams winning have automated quality measurement.Implement cost controls now. Don&#8217;t be the &#8220;$60K surprise&#8221; guy from last month&#8217;s post.If you&#8217;re using AI at work:Get good at prompting. This is a career skill now.Know where AI fails. The person who knows when NOT to trust AI is more valuable than the person who uses it for everything.Document what&#8217;s AI-assisted. &#8220;Was this AI-generated?&#8221; is a question you&#8217;ll be asked.If you&#8217;re worried about your job:Some jobs are getting automated (data entry, basic support, routine content). The number of humans needed is shrinking.Most jobs are getting augmented. The developer with AI does 10x the work. Same for lawyers, analysts, marketers.The strategy: Become the person who&#8217;s 10x more productive WITH AI, not the person being replaced BY someone who is.A couple of billion dollar problems to solve.We&#8217;re running out of training data. The internet is increasingly AI-generated slop. The next generation of models needs synthetic data, private data deals, or new architectures. This might slow progress more than people expect.Energy is becoming a real constraint. Training frontier models requires power-plant-level electricity. Microsoft is restarting Three Mile Island. Not a joke. They literally need the power.We don&#8217;t know how to measure progress. Benchmarks are saturated and gamed. We might be overestimating progress in some areas and underestimating it in others. We genuinely don&#8217;t know.Analogies, because everyone loves analogies.2025 was AI going from &#8220;impressive demo&#8221; to &#8220;actual infrastructure.&#8221;2026 is figuring out what to build with that infrastructure.The hype is recalibrating. The technology is maturing. The hard work of making AI genuinely useful, reliably, affordably, safely is just starting.The winners won&#8217;t have the best models. They&#8217;ll have the best applications.Next Week: WTF is MCP (Model Context Protocol)?Anthropic dropped this thing called MCP and everyone&#8217;s pretending they understand it. &#8220;It&#8217;s like USB for AI!&#8221; Cool, that explains nothing.MCP is how you connect AI to your actual stuff&#8212;databases, APIs, files, tools&#8212;without writing janky integration code for every model. It&#8217;s either the future of AI tooling or another standard that dies as soon as the next best thing comes along.We&#8217;ll cover what it actually is, why it matters (or doesn&#8217;t), and how to set it up without losing your mind.See you next Wednesday &#129310;pls subscribe
